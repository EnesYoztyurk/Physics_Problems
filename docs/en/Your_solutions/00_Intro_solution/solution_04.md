## 4. Rearranging Formulas (Step-by-Step Solution)

We are given the formula for the period of a simple pendulum:

$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

**Goal:** Rearrange the equation to isolate $g$ (acceleration due to gravity).

---

## Step-by-Step Derivation

### Step 1: Divide by $2\pi$
To begin isolating the square root term, we divide both sides of the equation by $2\pi$:

$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

### Step 2: Square both sides
To eliminate the square root, we square every term on both sides of the equation:

$$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \left(\sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}\right)^2$$

This simplifies to:

$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

### Step 3: Use cross-multiplication (or reciprocal)
To get $g$ out of the denominator, we can multiply both sides by $g$ and then multiply by the reciprocal of the left side:

$$g \cdot \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L$$

Now, multiply by $\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$:

$$g = L \cdot \frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}$$

---

## Final Formula

The rearranged formula for $g$ is:

$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

**Answer:**
$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$
