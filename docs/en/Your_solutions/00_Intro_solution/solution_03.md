## 3. Proportionality (Detailed Step-by-Step Solution)

In this problem, we analyze how changes in physical variables affect the result of a power-law equation using the Universal Law of Gravitation:

$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

---

## Given Conditions
We need to determine the change in force $F$ when:
1.  **Distance $r$ is doubled:** $r_{new} = 2r$
2.  **Mass $m_1$ is halved:** $m_{1, new} = \frac{1}{2} m_1$
3.  **Mass $m_2$ is halved:** $m_{2, new} = \frac{1}{2} m_2$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Set up the initial force equation
Let the original force be $F_{initial}$:
$$F_{initial} = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

### Step 2: Substitute the new values into the formula
Now, we substitute the modified variables into the gravitational equation to find the new force $F_{new}$:

$$F_{new} = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2} m_1) (\frac{1}{2} m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

### Step 3: Simplify the numerator
Multiply the mass factors in the numerator:
$$\frac{1}{2} m_1 \cdot \frac{1}{2} m_2 = \frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2$$

### Step 4: Simplify the denominator
Apply the square to the new distance:
$$(2r)^2 = 4r^2$$

### Step 5: Combine and calculate the final factor
Substitute the simplified terms back into the $F_{new}$ equation:

$$F_{new} = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}$$

To find the ratio, we pull the constants out:
$$F_{new} = \left( \frac{1/4}{4} \right) \cdot G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
$$F_{new} = \frac{1}{16} \cdot G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

---

## Conclusion
By comparing $F_{new}$ to $F_{initial}$, we find:
$$F_{new} = \frac{1}{16} F_{initial}$$

**Final Answer:**
The gravitational force $F$ decreases by a factor of **16**.
