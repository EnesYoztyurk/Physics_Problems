## 2. Systems of Equations (Detailed Step-by-Step Solution)

We are asked to find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy the following linear system:

1)  $2x + 3y = 12$
2)  $x - y = 1$

---

## Methodology: Elimination Method

To solve this system efficiently, we will use the **Elimination Method** by aligning the coefficients of $y$.

### Step 1: Prepare the equations
We want to eliminate $y$. To do this, we multiply the second equation by **3** so that the coefficients of $y$ in both equations are opposites ($+3$ and $-3$).

* Equation 1: $2x + 3y = 12$
* Equation 2 (Multiplied by 3): $3(x - y) = 3(1) \implies 3x - 3y = 3$

### Step 2: Add the equations together
Now, we add the two equations to eliminate the $y$ variable:

$$
\begin{aligned}
(2x + 3y) & = 12 \\
+ \quad (3x - 3y) & = 3 \\
\hline
5x + 0 & = 15
\end{aligned}
$$

### Step 3: Solve for $x$
Divide both sides by 5:

$$
5x = 15 \implies x = \frac{15}{5} \implies x = 3
$$

### Step 4: Substitute $x$ back into an original equation to find $y$
We will use Equation 2 ($x - y = 1$) as it is the simplest:

$$
3 - y = 1
$$

Subtract 3 from both sides:
$$
-y = 1 - 3 \implies -y = -2
$$

Multiply by $-1$:
$$
y = 2
$$

---

## Verification (Double Check)
Let's plug $x = 3$ and $y = 2$ into the first equation to verify:
$$2(3) + 3(2) = 6 + 6 = 12$$
The solution is correct.

**Final Answer:**
$$x = 3, \quad y = 2$$
