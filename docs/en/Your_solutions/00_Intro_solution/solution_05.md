## 5. Trigonometry (Detailed Step-by-Step Solution)

In this problem, we are given a vector $\vec{A}$ with a specific magnitude and direction, and we need to find its scalar components along the horizontal ($x$) and vertical ($y$) axes.

**Given:**
* Magnitude of the vector: $|\vec{A}| = 15$
* Angle with the horizontal axis: $\theta = 60^\circ$

---

## Methodology: Vector Decomposition

To find the components, we use the trigonometric definitions of sine and cosine for a right-angled triangle:

1.  **Horizontal Component ($A_x$):** $A_x = |\vec{A}| \cos(\theta)$
2.  **Vertical Component ($A_y$):** $A_y = |\vec{A}| \sin(\theta)$

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Calculate the Horizontal Component ($A_x$)
Substitute the given values into the cosine formula:

$$
A_x = 15 \cdot \cos(60^\circ)
$$

Since $\cos(60^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$
A_x = 15 \cdot 0.5 = 7.5
$$

### Step 2: Calculate the Vertical Component ($A_y$)
Substitute the given values into the sine formula:

$$
A_y = 15 \cdot \sin(60^\circ)
$$

Since $\sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$:
$$
A_y = 15 \cdot 0.866 \approx 12.99
$$

---

## Final Result

The components of vector $\vec{A}$ are:
* **Horizontal component ($A_x$):** $7.5$
* **Vertical component ($A_y$):** $12.99$ (or $\frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}$)

**Vector notation:**
$$\vec{A} = [7.5,\, 12.99]$$
