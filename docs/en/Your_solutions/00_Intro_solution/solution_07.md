## 7. Logic & Series (Step-by-Step Solution)

**The Problem:**
A bicycle is 10 meters from a wall and moves towards it at $1 \text{ m/s}$. A fly starts from the bicycle and flies towards the wall at $2 \text{ m/s}$, turning back and forth between the wall and the bicycle until it is crushed. What is the total distance the fly travels?

---

## Methodology: Time-Based Logical Approach

While this problem can be represented as an infinite geometric series, the simplest and most elegant way to solve it is by determining the **total time** the fly is in the air.

1.  Find the time it takes for the bicycle to reach the wall.
2.  Multiply that time by the fly's constant speed.

---

## Step-by-Step Solution

### Step 1: Calculate the total time until impact
The bicycle is 10 meters away and travels at a constant speed of $1 \text{ m/s}$. The time ($t$) until it hits the wall is:

$$t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{10 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ m/s}} = 10 \text{ seconds}$$

The fly will be flying back and forth for exactly this duration.

### Step 2: Calculate the fly's total distance
The fly moves at a constant speed of $2 \text{ m/s}$ during those 10 seconds. Its path is complex, but its speed is constant:

$$\text{Total Distance} = \text{Speed}_{\text{fly}} \times t$$
$$\text{Total Distance} = 2 \text{ m/s} \times 10 \text{ s} = 20 \text{ meters}$$

---

## Final Result

Regardless of how many times the fly turns back and forth, it spends 10 seconds in the air at a constant speed of $2 \text{ m/s}$.

**Answer:**
The total distance the fly travels is **20 meters**.
